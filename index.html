Intel's Sustainability Journey

A timeline of key goals and milestones as Intel works toward a lower‑carbon, more responsible future in semiconductor design and manufacturing.

2020

Renewable Momentum

Intel expands renewable electricity purchases and sets ambitious 2030 goals.

Focus areas include energy efficiency, greenhouse gas reductions, and water stewardship programs across global sites.

2021

Greener Manufacturing

Pilot projects reduce process emissions and improve material reuse.

Supplier collaboration ramps up to track carbon, and design teams prioritize power‑efficient chips.

2022

Efficient Computing

Design guidance helps customers build energy‑aware systems at scale.

Documentation and reference designs highlight performance‑per‑watt and thermal best practices.

2023

Materials & Water

Progress continues on reclaiming water and sourcing safer chemistries.

Sites invest in on‑site water restoration and partner on circular material streams.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel convenes partners to accelerate low‑carbon manufacturing.

Suppliers, researchers, and policy leaders share roadmaps for next‑gen processes and greener fabs.

2025

Design for Efficiency

Expanded guidance for platform‑level power management and cooling.

Open resources demonstrate telemetry‑driven savings and thermal design patterns.

2026

Lower‑Carbon Supply Chain

Deeper supplier engagement improves transparency and reporting.

Shared targets and tools help reduce upstream emissions for silicon and packaging.

2027

Circularity in Focus

Programs to extend product life and reclaim materials keep growing.

From packaging to equipment, more parts are designed for reuse, repair, and recycling.

2030 Goal

Toward Net Positive

Intel targets ambitious 2030 objectives across energy, water, and waste.

These goals guide ongoing investments in clean energy, water restoration, and sustainable operations worldwide.
